Ignite Curiosity. Empower Learners.

Founded by Tolu Adewuyi, Philip Thang, and Gaby Martinez Gil, SparkBox Initiative is a hands-on STEM education initiative that delivers the fundamentals of engineering and science to students, especially in underfunded or overlooked schools, through engaging kits ("Sparkboxes") and guided lessons.

Why SparkBox?

We believe every student deserves access to high-quality STEM education. Too many schools lack the resources to teach science through hands-on exploration. That's where SparkBox comes in.

Our kits bridge the gap between theory and practice, turning abstract concepts into tangible experiences. We focus on equity, innovation, and fun—because learning should spark joy.

Our Founders

Tolu Adewuyi

Aspiring Electrical Engineer

Tolu Adewuyi is a rising junior and electrical engineering enthusiast dedicated to making STEM education accessible and engaging. As the founder of SparkBox Initiative, Tolu combines a passion for hands-on learning with a commitment to empowering underserved students through innovative, reusable science kits. Beyond SparkBox, Tolu runs the YouTube channel Archrono, where entertaining visuals bring complex engineering concepts to life. Driven by a vision to ignite curiosity and build future innovators, Tolu balances academic rigor with impactful outreach, proving that creativity and engineering go hand in hand.

Philip Thang

Engineer & Maker

Philip's engineering background and love for hands-on building led him to create low-cost, high-impact learning tools that make complex concepts tangible and fun for young learners.

Gaby Martinez Gil

Community Organizer

Gaby connects SparkBox with schools and communities that need it most. Her outreach ensures our kits reach students in underfunded schools who would otherwise miss out on STEM experiences.
